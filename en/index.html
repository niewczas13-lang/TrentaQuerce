Our story

Living and working close to nature

Mariabarbara Conti, an agronomist specialised in oenology, made her dream of living and working close to nature come true with the Trenta Querce farm. The estate produces wine, olive oil and fruit in a land the Etruscans and Romans chose more than two thousand years ago to farm — and to enjoy, just like you, a relaxing holiday.

Faithful to the traditions of this land, with every comfort of modern life. Mariabarbara Conti — owner, agronomist & winemaker

The winery

The "30" wines

One grape, Merlot, in three interpretations. Hand-picked, temperature-controlled winemaking, no insecticides and no herbicides: only copper and sulphur in the vineyard.

Why "30"? A story that begins with thirty oaks

When Mariabarbara Conti bought this land in 2004 she was thirty years old — and thirty oak trees welcomed her at the entrance of the estate. That double "30" gave the farm its name and her wines their label.

Before becoming a winemaker in her own right, Mariabarbara worked with some of central Italy's most prestigious estates: Trappolini, Avignonesi, and five years as technical director at Antinori's Castello della Sala.

From clay soils rich in minerals and marine fossils, three different Merlot clones give life — from a single vineyard — to a classic-method sparkling, a rosé and an age-worthy red.

Red · Merlot IGT Umbria

"30" Merlot

Hand-picked in small crates, macerated in steel at a controlled temperature below 28 °C. 10% of the wine is aged in French oak barriques, where it completes malolactic fermentation; after blending, about 2 months of bottle ageing.

Part of the Merlot also becomes our wine reduction: a dense nectar for cheeses, meat and salads.

Rosé · 100% Merlot

"30" Rosato

Grapes hand-picked in August, soft-pressed in a vertical press with a couple of hours on the skins. After a day of cooling at 10 °C, only the cleanest fraction of the must ferments below 18 °C. About 2 months of bottle ageing.

Where we are · Getting here

In Italy's green heart

On a panoramic hill among the woods, vineyards and olive groves of the lower Tiber valley, where Umbria, Lazio and Tuscany meet. A short walk away: the remains of the Roman villa of Poggio Gramignano and the village of Lugnano, the ancient Lucus Jani.

Open in Google Maps Open in Apple Maps

By car

A1 motorway Rome–Florence, exit Attigliano, then follow the SP33 provincial road towards Lugnano for 5 km: the sign for the agriturismo is on your left.

Sat-nav: "Via della Barca" · N 42°33.030' · E 012°18.530'

By train

Attigliano-Bomarzo station is 5 km away, on the Florence–Rome line. Rome is about 50 minutes: perfect for a car-free day trip to the capital.

By plane

Rome Fiumicino is about 110 km away (then train or rental car), or Perugia "San Francesco d'Assisi" airport at about 65 km.
